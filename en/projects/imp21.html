Shanghai Expo 2010 – Uzbekistan Pavilion

Project Description

Project name

Shanghai Expo 2010 – Uzbekistan Pavilion

Client

Uzbekistan Expo Center

Project location

Expo Park Shanghai 2010 Expo – Zone A

Project area

1000㎡

Investment

USD 993,000

Project duration

December 2009 - April 2010

Project Scope

Construction Design; Permitting, Fit-Out; Display Installation; Multi-Media Display Design and Installation; Facility Maintenance.

PDF Download


The theme of Uzbekistan Pavilion is "The intersection of culture ". The pavilion is divided into six zones: "Look to the future of the city ", "Harmonious city, harmonious village ", "Ancient and forever-young Tashkent ", "The city of pursuing progress and innovation", "Traditional and modern way of life in Uzbekistan" and "Uzbekistan is a great potential tourist attraction ". In the pavilion also set up an office, sale area and a small kitchen.

Nomura Construction completed all construction design work. Because of the delay of early concept design, Uzbekistan Pavilion is among the last batch of breaking-in pavilions with very limited construction time. Nomura Construction called together a large number of suppliers and workers to work day and night and finally completed construction and passed acceptance before the opening of Expo. The pavilion successfully opened on May 1st, 2010. On the top of pavilion set a crane-style sculpture. It is the only one to make sculpture on the top of pavilion at Shanghai World Expo. Nomura’s engineers did accurate calculation to the installation structure to ensure safety during the typhoon period.

The Pavilion facade is decorated with strong ethnic characteristics, which perfectly reproduced the style of Uzbekistan traditional architecture. Nomura put forward specific installation scheme and construction drawings of the decoration’s installation in a very short period of time to ensure that the project be completed on schedule.

Above the pavilion entrance is a large outdoor LED screen playing movies of Uzbekistan scenery introduction. Nomura Construction completed professional design for it in a very short period of time and coordinated with LED screen suppliers and installers to ensure the production, transportation, installation and commissioning work to be completed on time.

In the central hall there is a sculpture fountain, above which is a multimedia projection dome. Nomura’s artists and vendors work day and night with great enthusiasm to ensure the pavilion can be open on time. In the pavilion there are rockeries and traditional arts and crafts and clothing of Uzbekistan characteristics. The display of sand table and the use of multimedia technology took the visitors to travel into a virtual Shiva city tour.
