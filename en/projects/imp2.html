Shanghai Expo 2010 - Togo Pavilion

Project Description

Project name

Shanghai Expo 2010 - Togo Pavilion

Client

Togo ministry of commerce

Project location

Expo Park Shanghai 2010 Expo – Zone C, inside the Pacific Joint Pavilion

Project area

350㎡

Investment

USD 600,000

Project duration

Sep, 2009-April, 2010

Project Scope

Construction Design; Permitting, Fit-Out; Display Installation; Multi-Media Display Design and Installation Facility maintenance, Operation management

PDF Download


Togo pavilion is located inside the Pacific joint pavilion, whose theme is: a Prosperity of Urban Economy “Prosperity of Urban Economy, is composed by 4 areas: facade decoration, image and text area, central sculpture, city model, tells the story of capital Lome ‘s construction concept, and the experience of urban prosperity, to discuss the problems during the urbanization of developing countries and make Lome become a better city in future.

Nomura completed all the construction drawings. Togo pavilion decoration takes the traditional round ceiling hut as its style and decorates with red clay, fabrics and thatch, looks like castle, and take kinds of handcrafts that stand for Togo’s rich culture for ornaments, such as Togo local carpets, canvas, mask and sculpture. As the normal cloth and thatch cannot meet the fire requirements, Nomura communicated with Expo Bureau with having some treatment to the thatch and cloth, and finally got the fire department approval.

In the central of Togo pavilion, there’s stood a statue of dove that stand for peace, which was completed by the students that hired from Art College by Nomura. Togo woodcarving mask is another light point—under the wall light box, there ‘s row of mask, the prototype mostly are local ancient tribal chief, or the heroes in legend. Nomura seek for those who can finish the woodcarving work person from other provinces. The director of Togo pavilion gave high praise to Nomura with a thanks letter.
