Shanghai Expo 2010 - Kyrgyz Pavilion

Project Description

Project name

Shanghai Expo 2010 - Kyrgyz Pavilion

Client

Kyrgyz Industrial and Commercial Committee

Project location

Expo Park Shanghai 2010 Expo – Zone A

Project area

350㎡

Investment

USD 600,000

Project duration

October 2009 - April 2010

Project Scope

Construction Design; Permitting, Fit-Out; Display Installation; Multi-Media Display Design and Installation; Facility Maintenance and Pavilion Operation Management.

PDF Download


The theme of Kyrgyz Pavilion is "Bishkek – the city open to the world". The pavilion introduces Kyrgyz national culture, history, customs and tells the harmonious development of Bishkek economy, culture, tourism through films, posters, slides, exhibits and cultural activities. The display design took the element of the heaven, the earth and the sun. The top of the pavilion is a multimedia dome, playing films about Kyrgyz historical epic. Around the pavilion installed multimedia simulation devices for visitors’ use. We built a real Mongolian yurt at the entrance of the pavilion and produced Kyrgyz local wall around. In the central area showed “stone sculpture” of Kyrgyz history culture. In display area showed herdsman living props and equestrian appliances etc. There are store area and an office in the pavilion.

Nomura Construction was appointed as the Design, Build and Operation Contractor in October 2009. Nomura’s design team carried the Concept through the Scheme Design to final Construction Design. In the stage of decoration we need to lift the dome of steel structure. Nomura Construction solved the structure and installation problems of the dome. Since the installation will be carried out inside the pavilion, while the dome's size is nearly equal to the size of the pavilion’s ceiling, it means the design of installation method and the use of hoisting tool are restricted within the 350 square meters’ building, there is almost no room to maneuver. Nomura Construction accurately completed the assembly of this complex work. In order to show the visitors Kyrgyz architectural features, Nomura Construction sent staff to make a special inspection of their local wall, and then used mud and grass- the country's most primitive production process to duplicate the wall into the pavilion.

To reflect the theme of the pavilion, Nomura Construction hired teachers and students of the art college to complete the exhibits and accurately created the pavilion's theme atmosphere. The use of many high technology and multimedia precisely represented the theme of the pavilion - "Bishkek – the city open to the world ".

Nomura Construction was responsible for the pavilion’s 184 days operation & management work including organizing the pavilion’s National Day activities. Services like staff recruitment and training, reception arrangement, security measures and daily facility maintenance were all provided by Nomura Construction.
