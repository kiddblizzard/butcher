Shanghai Expo 2010 - Tajikistan Pavilion

Project Description

Project name

Shanghai Expo 2010 - Tajikistan Pavilion

Client

Tajik Industrial and Commercial Committee

Project location

Expo Park Shanghai 2010 Expo – Zone A

Project area

350㎡

Investment

USD 600,000

Project duration

October 2009 - April 2010

Project Scope

Construction Design; Permitting, Fit-Out; Display Installation; Multi-Media Display Design and Installation; Facility Maintenance and Pavilion Operation Management.

PDF Download


The theme of Tajikistan Pavilion is “The city changes and the good life”. Nomura Construction duplicated the famous Tajik Castle design element to make the entrance door. The exhibition is divided into three themed areas: The famous Pamir mountain model and waterfall landscape stand for the country's rich hydropower resources. In the lake at the mountain foot which is actually a pool is the simulation of Tajik local animals and plants. The exquisite models of ancient city and fortress reflect Tajik culture of different ages. We show the visitors ordinary Tajik family life and their leisure scene - beautifully carved pillars, grape arbor decorated with green leaves, national blankets and chairs, which create a pleasant courtyard.

Nomura construction completed all construction drawings, production of castle facade, interior decoration and display work. We made the snow mountain model, a water circulation system is set up to show spectacular scenes of the country’s falls. The beautifully carved artwork highlighted the pavilion. Wood carvings can be seen everywhere from the entrance to each corner of the pavilion. These wood carvings were specially customized by Nomura. The patterns on wood carvings are traditional Tajik ones, which reflected civilized life of Tajik cities.

Nomura Construction was responsible for the pavilion’s 184 days operation & management work including organizing the pavilion’s National Day activities. Services like staff recruitment and training, reception arrangement, security measures and daily facility maintenance were all provided by Nomura Construction.
